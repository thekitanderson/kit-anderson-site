01 — Identity
WHO IS
KIT ANDERSON

He's been writing on walls since 1979. Author of three novels and three crime novellas. Director of an action sports cult movie. Gold Medal-winning producer. Phantogram's first roadie. Art Director for the Million Family March and the inaugural Napa Valley Film Festival. Visual artist working in graphite, paint, sculpture, and mixed media. Running for Mayor of Lake Tahoe on a platform of free Porsches. He treats all of it with equal regard.

Full name
Kit Anderson
Always "Kit Anderson" in full. Never "Kit" alone in formal contexts, never "K. Anderson." The full name is the brand.
Pronouns
He / Him
Refer to Kit as he/him. Not she. Not they. He.
Primary descriptor
Author, Artist, and Amateur Stuntman
Use in full. The Oxford comma is load-bearing. "Amateur Stuntman" is not a joke — it's a values statement.
Base of operations
Truckee, California
Not "near Lake Tahoe." Not "the Sierra Nevada." Truckee, California. Always specific.
Operating model
Private media kitchen
Signals independence, craft, plurality, and a refusal to be categorized. Use it.
Core disciplines
Writing · Movies & Video · Visual Art
Plus snowboarding, skating, Porsche driving — pursuits treated with equal artistic regard. The lifestyle is part of the work.

02 — Logo
THE MARK

A hand-lettered signature with a heart integrated into the letterforms. Raw, personal, irreproducible. Three official variants. Always use the original artwork files — never attempt to recreate it digitally.

PrimaryOn White / Light
ReversedOn Black
EditorialOn Dark
Usage rules
✓
Do

Use the original artwork files only. The mark works at any scale as long as the file is used as-is.

✕
Don't

Never stretch, skew, rotate, or attempt to recreate the mark digitally. The hand-lettered quality is the point.

✓
Do

Maintain clear space equal to the width of the "K" on all four sides.

✕
Don't

Never place the mark on mid-tone, patterned, or busy backgrounds. High contrast only.

✓
Do

The heart is inseparable from the letterforms. Keep it intact in every use.

✕
Don't

Never recolor the mark. Always black on light, white/light on dark. Never accent colors.

05 — Voice & Messaging
HOW KIT
SPEAKS

The voice is the work. Deadpan without being cold. Serious about the Art, not about himself. Fake celebrity endorsements deployed with complete conviction. The Oxford comma always in place. Never explains the joke.

SERIOUS
About the work, not himself
The Art, the books, and the movies are treated with genuine craft and complete earnestness
Never self-deprecating about quality — only about the premise
Credentials stated plainly: Gold Medal. Art Director. Cult movie. No hedging.
DEADPAN
Committed to the bit, always
The joke is never announced — it's in the structure, the fine print, the asterisk
Fake quotes from dead celebrities delivered without winking at the camera
"Nothing is real" is both a punchline and a genuine artistic statement. Both are true.
DIRECT
Short sentences. No filler.
Statements, not explanations. Declarations, not pitches.
The bio doesn't say "aspiring" or "passionate about." It says what he did and when.
Comfortable with fragments. Comfortable with silence.
PLURAL
Many things at once, unapologetically
Does not choose between novelist, moviemaker, visual Artist, skateboarder, Porsche driver
The plurality is the brand — "private media kitchen" holds it all
No hierarchy between disciplines. A novella and a Porsche road video get equal billing.
House style
Art / Artist
Always capitalized. Art, Artist, Artwork. Signals that Kit treats making as a serious discipline, not a hobby.
Movies vs. film
Never "film." Movies (features), videos (YouTube/short-form), motion pictures (formal). "Film" is not Kit's word.
Oxford comma
Always. "Author, Artist, and Amateur Stuntman" — that comma before "and" is load-bearing. Never omit it.
Full name
Always "Kit Anderson" in full in formal contexts. Never "Kit" alone, never "K. Anderson," never "Anderson" alone.
Sentence case
Running copy is always sentence case — first word and proper nouns only. Not title case.
Pronouns
He/him. Always. Not she, not they.
Nothing is real.
The period is part of it. Always with the full stop. The punctuation is intentional.
Truckee, California
Not "near Lake Tahoe." Not "the Sierra Nevada." Truckee, California. Always specific.
Anderson — never broken
"Anderson" must always appear whole on a single line. "Kit" may stand alone above. Never wraps, never breaks, never splits. Fix the layout before you break "Anderson."

07 — The North Star
WIN THE
WIKIPEDIA
PAGE

Current holding line: "Kit Anderson does not have a Wikipedia page. This is better." That's the interim position. The real goal is to make it no longer true. The six-site network is the SEO infrastructure that gets him there.

0
WIKIPEDIA PAGES CURRENTLY HELD

Wikipedia requires notability — coverage in reliable, independent, secondary sources. Every site is a SEO node. Every node is a link. Every link is a signal.

What the network is building toward:

01Owning the SERP. When you search "Kit Anderson," every result on page one should be Kit Anderson. Six domains make this possible.
02IMDb as an anchor. Kit already has an IMDb page (nm3556873). Movie credits are among the strongest notability signals Wikipedia accepts.
03Published books via Ingram. Multiple titles in wide distribution. Each one is a citable, verifiable fact.
04Awards and credits. Gold Medal, Park City Film Music Festival. Art Director, inaugural Napa Valley Film Festival. Art Director, Million Family March.
05Press coverage — the missing piece. The Mayor campaign and Jerk Calhoun are designed to be press-friendly — absurdist, self-contained, quotable. That's how you get the articles that make the Wikipedia page possible.
